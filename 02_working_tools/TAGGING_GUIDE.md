# SurvivingSOGICE — Tagging Guide: Hard Distinctions

*Reference document for researcher and research assistant*
*Use this when unsure which tag applies — these are the calls that require judgment*

## EVIDENCE — What kind of knowledge claim is being made?

Evidence tags classify **who is making the claim and what epistemic status it has** — not whether you agree with it. The question is: what does this source present itself as?

---

### The hardest pair: `Evidence: Organization-Claim` vs `Evidence: Propaganda`

This is the most common judgment call. The difference is **intent and register**, not content.

| | Organization-Claim | Propaganda |
|---|---|---|
| **Tone** | Measured, institutional, policy-like | Emotionally charged, mobilising |
| **Goal** | Persuade professionals, policymakers, lawmakers | Persuade the general public, recruit, inflame |
| **Format** | Report, position statement, white paper, submission | Video, campaign, pamphlet, social media, documentary |
| **Audience** | Legislators, therapists, parents, church leaders | General public, congregation, online followers |
| **Self-presentation** | "We represent..." / "Our research shows..." | "The truth they don't want you to know..." |

**Practical rule:**
- A PDF submission to a parliamentary committee by IFTCC opposing a conversion therapy ban → `Organization-Claim`
- A YouTube video by X-Out-Loud showing ex-gay testimonies with a call to action → `Propaganda`
- A press release by Family Research Council claiming SOGICE bans violate religious freedom → `Organization-Claim`
- A TikTok hashtag campaign promoting #overcominghomosexuality → `Propaganda`

**The edge case:** A "report" that looks professional but uses inflammatory language and is designed for mass circulation. Ask: *would a therapist or lawmaker cite this as evidence, or would a congregation member share it on Facebook?* If the latter → `Propaganda`.

### Full Evidence taxonomy

**`Evidence: Academic`**
Peer-reviewed journals, university press books, conference papers with methodology. Must have gone through scholarly review. Includes *pro-SOGICE* pseudo-journals (e.g. Journal of Human Sexuality) — they present themselves as academic even if the science is contested.
*Ask: Does it have citations, methodology, and peer review?*

**`Evidence: Government`**
Official state documents: parliamentary reports, government inquiries, ministry publications, court judgments, legislative texts. Neutral on whether the government supports or opposes SOGICE.
*Ask: Was this produced by a state institution in an official capacity?*

**`Evidence: NGO`**
Reports and statements from civil society organisations — ILGA-Europe, GATE, Council of Europe, Amnesty, NCLR. These are advocacy organisations but produce evidence-quality research with methodology.
*Ask: Does this org produce research that gets cited in policy, even if it has a position?*

**`Evidence: Journalism`**
News articles, investigative reporting, documentary journalism. The journalist is synthesising and reporting, not making a primary claim.
*Ask: Is someone reporting on SOGICE, rather than promoting or opposing it directly?*

**`Evidence: Organization-Claim`**
An organisation stating its position, policy, or beliefs in a professional register. IFTCC submissions, NARTH position papers, Core Issues Trust policy briefings, but also Stonewall policy documents or ILGA statements. Both pro- and anti-SOGICE orgs make organisation-claims.
*Ask: Is an org speaking in an official, professional voice about its position?*

**`Evidence: Propaganda`**
Content designed to emotionally persuade, recruit, or mobilise — not to inform. Always promotional. Includes testimonial videos, campaign materials, revival content, social media campaigns.
*Ask: Is this designed to move people emotionally and change their behaviour?*

**`Evidence: Public Social Media`**
Posts, threads, comments on social platforms — Twitter/X, Facebook, Reddit, TikTok, Instagram. The claim is made by an individual or organisation in a public social media context, without the editorial register of a report or statement.
*Ask: Is this a social media post rather than a produced document?*

**`Evidence: Testimony`**
First-person accounts — survivor testimonies, ex-gay testimonies, personal narratives. The claim is experiential, not institutional. Includes both critical survivor accounts and promotional ex-gay testimonies.
*Ask: Is one person speaking from personal experience?*

**`Evidence: Unverified`**
Content where the source, authorship, or authenticity cannot be confirmed. Screenshots without context, anonymous posts, documents of unclear provenance.
*Ask: Can you confirm who made this and where it originally came from?*

## TACTICS — What rhetorical move is being made?

Tactics tags are the **most important field**. They classify *how* SOGICE ideology is being communicated, not just *what* is being said. A single source can use multiple tactics simultaneously — be generous here.

---

### The hardest pairs

**`Tactic: Rebranding-SOGICE` vs `Tactic: Policy-Resistance-Frame`**

These often appear together but target different things.

- **Rebranding** is about *language* — renaming conversion therapy to avoid the label. The goal is to make SOGICE sound neutral or therapeutic. "Congruence therapy", "identity exploration", "sexual wholeness ministry" are all rebranding moves.
- **Policy-Resistance** is about *law* — opposing bans on conversion therapy by framing them as attacks on freedom, choice, or rights. The argument is "you shouldn't be able to ban this."

*A source can do both: "We offer identity exploration therapy [rebranding] and oppose government bans on therapeutic choice [policy-resistance]."*

**`Tactic: False-Scientific-Authority` vs `Tactic: Social-Contagion-Myth`**

- **False-Scientific-Authority** is the general move of misusing science — citing cherry-picked studies, inventing pseudo-diagnoses, weaponising neuroplasticity research, claiming clinical expertise without it.
- **Social-Contagion-Myth** is one *specific* false scientific claim: that LGBTQ+ identities spread peer-to-peer like a trend or infection.

*Social-Contagion-Myth is almost always also False-Scientific-Authority, but False-Scientific-Authority covers much more ground (ROGD, attachment disorder theory, aversion therapy claims, etc.).*

**`Tactic: Anti-Gender Narrative` vs `Tactic: Anti-LGBT Conspiracy`**

- **Anti-Gender Narrative** is the European far-right/Catholic frame that positions LGBTQ+ rights as "gender ideology" — an ideological project being imposed on society, destroying natural gender and family. It's abstract and structural ("gender ideology is taking over").
- **Anti-LGBT Conspiracy** is more personal and threatening — claiming coordinated actors are specifically targeting children, families, or Christians ("they're coming for our kids", "indoctrination in schools", "global LGBTQ+ agenda").

*Anti-Gender is ideological critique; Anti-LGBT Conspiracy is a threat narrative. Norwegian kjønnsideologi content is usually Anti-Gender; grooming panic content is usually Anti-LGBT Conspiracy.*

**`Tactic: Anti-Trans Rhetoric` vs `Tactic: ROGD-Frame` vs `Tactic: Detrans-Propaganda`**

These are all anti-trans but target different things:

- **Anti-Trans Rhetoric** is the broad category — delegitimising trans identity, attacking gender-affirming care, denying that trans people exist as they say they do.
- **ROGD-Frame** is a specific mechanism: claiming that trans identity in young people is caused by social contagion / peer pressure / parental influence, using the debunked "Rapid Onset Gender Dysphoria" pseudo-diagnosis.
- **Detrans-Propaganda** weaponises stories of people who detransitioned — not to support them, but to argue that all transition is a mistake and care should be restricted.

*A source can use all three: "Trans identity isn't real [Anti-Trans], it spreads through social media [ROGD], and the regret is epidemic [Detrans-Propaganda]."*

**`Tactic: Gender-Essentialism` vs `Tactic: Sex-Rejection-Frame`**

- **Gender-Essentialism** is the positive claim: biological sex is fixed, binary, and determines gender. "True masculinity", "God created male and female", "biology is destiny."
- **Sex-Rejection-Frame** is the negative framing of transition specifically: transition is described as a *rejection* of one's "true" biological sex — a form of self-harm or self-denial.

*Gender-Essentialism sets the premise; Sex-Rejection-Frame applies it to trans people specifically.*

## OTHER HARD CALLS

### `Function: Euphemism` vs `Term: [specific term]`

**Function** tags describe what the *whole source* is doing. **Term** tags mark specific *phrases* used promotionally in the source.

A source that extensively uses "SSA" language throughout should get:
- `Term: SSA` (the specific phrase is used promotionally)
- `Function: Euphemism` (the overall discourse function of the source)

A source that only mentions SSA once in passing while critiquing it gets *neither*.

---

### When to use `needs_review: true`

Use sparingly — only when you are genuinely uncertain about a classification that affects the research value of the record. Good reasons:
- You can't confirm which country the organisation is from
- The source could be either Organisation-Claim or Propaganda and it matters
- You found a network connection you can't verify
- The source is in a language you can't fully read

Not a reason: you just weren't sure which of two tactics to use (tag both).

---

### Country tagging — the rule

**Tag the organisation's country, not the language of the text.**

- A Norwegian subtitle on a US video → `Country: USA`
- An Italian website promoting an American ministry's content → `Country: USA` (plus `Country: Italy` if the Italian org is also an actor)
- A French-language Belgian Catholic document → `Country: Belgium` (not France)
- Truly international orgs with no clear home country → `Country: International`
- EU institutions (Council of Europe, European Parliament reports) → `Country: EU`

When in doubt about country, use `Country: International` and flag with `needs_review: true`.
